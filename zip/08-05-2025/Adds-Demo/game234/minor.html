Bank Accounts for Adults

Complete guide to opening an account if you're 18+ years old

Types of Accounts Available

  • Savings Account - Earn interest on your deposits (2.5-7% interest depending on bank and balance)
  • Current Account - For business transactions (no interest, higher transaction limits, overdraft facility)
  • Salary Account - Special accounts for salaried individuals (often zero balance with premium benefits)
  • Senior Citizen Accounts - Higher interest rates (0.5% extra) for those above 60 with additional benefits
  • Women's Savings Account - Special accounts with lower minimum balance and additional benefits for women
  • Premium/Privilege Accounts - For high net-worth individuals with concierge services and premium benefits
  • Digital Savings Accounts - Fully digital accounts opened via mobile apps with video KYC
  • NRI Accounts - NRE (Non-Resident External), NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) and FCNR (Foreign Currency Non-Resident) accounts

Document Requirements

To open an account, you'll need:

  • Proof of Identity: Aadhaar (most preferred), PAN (mandatory for most accounts), Voter ID, Passport, Driving License
  • Proof of Address: Aadhaar, Utility bills (electricity/water/landline not older than 3 months), Rental agreement (registered), Property documents
  • Passport-size photographs: 2 recent photos with white background (some banks may require more)
  • PAN Card: Mandatory for all accounts except Jan Dhan accounts
  • Income Proof (for premium accounts): Salary slips, IT returns, Form 16
  • Business Proof (for current accounts): GST certificate, Shop Act license, Partnership deed

Important Notes:

  • All documents must be original for verification (copies will be attested and returned)
  • Name spelling should match exactly across all documents
  • Address proof documents should not be older than 3 months
  • For joint accounts, documents required from all account holders
  • Some banks may require additional documents based on account type

Interest Calculation Methods:

  • Daily Balance Method: Interest calculated on daily closing balance (most common)
  • Quarterly Compounding: Interest credited quarterly to your account
  • TDS Deduction: 10% TDS if interest exceeds ₹10,000 annually (submit Form 15G/H to avoid)
  • Senior Citizen Benefit: Additional 0.5% interest for accounts held by those above 60 years

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Choose your preferred bank and account type - Compare features, interest rates, charges and benefits
  2. Visit the bank branch or apply online - Most banks offer both options with digital KYC available
  3. Fill out the account opening form - Provide complete and accurate details
  4. Submit KYC documents - Originals for verification along with self-attested copies
  5. Make initial deposit - Amount varies by account type (can be as low as ₹500)
  6. Complete biometric verification - Fingerprint and iris scan for Aadhaar-linked accounts
  7. Receive your account details - Account number and welcome kit immediately or within 24 hours
  8. Activate internet banking - Set up online banking credentials
  9. Receive your debit card - Typically arrives within 7 working days via courier

  • Salary Accounts - For salaried individuals with employer tie-ups
